Our story

SmartRecipes was founded in 1967 by Gräsanden Gunnar II. At a time when food waste received little attention, Gunnar recognized that large amounts of edible food were being thrown away unnecessarily. He believed that better planning and more creative cooking could significantly reduce this waste. What started as a personal effort soon developed into a company built around responsible use of food.

The company has always been family run and the values established at its founding remain central today. Leadership was later passed to Gunnar's son, Gräsanden Göran, who continues to guide SmartRecipes with the same long term commitment. Under his direction, the company has grown while staying true to its original purpose.

Food waste has become a major global problem with serious environmental and economic consequences. SmartRecipes' long history gives the company a deep understanding of this issue. For our family, reducing food waste is not a passing concern but a responsibility that has been carried forward for generations.

Our goal

Food waste is one of the most serious challenges in the modern food system. Every year, large quantities of edible food are discarded, wasting natural resources and contributing to environmental damage. At SmartRecipes, our goal is to help reduce this waste by supporting smarter and more thoughtful cooking habits.

We create practical and flexible recipes that are designed to use ingredients people already have at home. This includes leftovers, aging produce, and pantry items that are often forgotten. Our recipes encourage simple adjustments rather than strict rules, making it easier for people to reduce waste in everyday cooking.

Our goal is also to change how people think about food. By promoting better planning, greater awareness, and respect for ingredients, we aim to support lasting habits that benefit both households and the environment. Reducing food waste should be achievable, meaningful, and enjoyable for everyone.
